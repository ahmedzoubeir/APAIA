**Question 1:**

According to MITRE ATT&CK for Enterprise, which technique ID relates to the failure to validate the origin of a communication channel?

**Options:**

A. T1134
B. T1110
C. T1112
D. T1121

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** T1110 (Origin Validation Error) describes the technique of not properly validating the source of a communication channel.

**Question 2:**

In the context of Cybersecurity Supply Chain Risk Management (C-SCRM), what is a common risk related to the reliance on third-party vendors?

**Options:**

A. Misconfiguration of critical assets
B. Introduction of compromised software
C. Unauthorized access to sensitive data
D. Denial of service attacks

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** C-SCRM focuses on addressing risks associated with the integration of third-party vendors, including the introduction of compromised software from untrustworthy sources.

**Question 3:**

Which of the following is a key feature of the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)?

**Options:**

A. It is exclusively designed for federal agencies
B. It focuses on privacy risk management
C. It supports continuous monitoring
D. It requires independent audits

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:** The RMF emphasizes continuous monitoring as a critical component of risk management, ensuring ongoing protection of systems.

**Question 4:**

According to NIST's Privacy Framework, what is the primary goal of data processing activities?

**Options:**

A. To minimize the collection of personal data
B. To prevent security incidents
C. To enable data monetization
D. To comply with regulatory requirements

**Correct Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The Privacy Framework prioritizes minimizing the collection and processing of personal data, safeguarding individuals' privacy rights.

**Question 5:**

Which of the following is a common consequence of an origin validation error vulnerability?

**Options:**

A. Gaining access to sensitive information
B. Escalating privileges to an unauthorized level
C. Executing arbitrary code on the target system
D. Disrupting system availability

**Correct Answer:** A

**Explanation:** An origin validation error vulnerability can allow an attacker to access data or resources that were not intended to be available, such as sensitive information or personally identifiable information (PII).
